Brand positioning · research-backed · 2026-07-17

For the people calendars quietly fail.

Positioning statement: For adults whose priorities reshuffle faster than a calendar can — ADHD brains, visual thinkers, default parents — Kachunk is the weekly commitment app that replaces schedules with touchable blocks and forgiving buckets, because a week can absorb a bad Tuesday and a timeline can't. Unlike streak-based habit trackers and subscription day-planners, Kachunk never shames a miss, never auto-resets, and never bills twice.

One-line distillation: "A week you can hold. Blocks you can move. Nothing you can break." — every clause is defensible with the citations below.

4 · The science hook (for press & store copy)

Motivation deficits in ADHD map to dopamine reward-pathway differences (Volkow et al.) — Kachunk pays out a tangible reward event per rep: a block you physically move, with sound and haptics.[17] Gamified immediate feedback shows large effects on sustained attention in ADHD RCTs (d≈0.95–1.25, in children — flag when quoting).[18] Clinical guidance for time blindness is to externalize time and progress — the floor of blocks IS the week, made visible.[8] And the weekly bucket is a forgiveness mechanic by construction: a missed Monday is invisible if Thursday catches it.[3],[5]

Claim discipline: "designed with ADHD brains in mind" ✓ · "treats/helps ADHD" ✗ (FTC + community backlash). The gamification RCT is pediatric; cite it as mechanism, not outcome promise.

5 · Personas

Late-Diagnosis Maya

28–38 · DIAGNOSED AT 31 · MARKETING MANAGER

One of the fastest-growing cohorts (women 30–49 diagnoses ~doubled 2020–22). Third abandoned planner this year; pays the "ADHD tax" (~$14K/yr excess cost per adult) and resents recurring bills. Found us on a "apps that don't shame you" thread.

Stays because: per-rep dopamine (block + sound + haptic), the week absorbs bad days, and she paid once — no renewal guilt.

Default-Parent Dana

35–44 · TWO KIDS · HOUSEHOLD COO

Among the 86% of working moms carrying the whole household; 72.6% of the family's cognitive labor is hers, and that share predicts her burnout. Her own gym/PT/friends have no calendar slot that survives a school pickup.

Stays because: "Gym 3× this week — any three times" fits a week she can't predict; one notification respects a full mind; the third block of the week feels like something done for her.

Feast-or-Famine Jules

24–34 · FREELANCE DESIGNER · CRUNCH/DRIFT CYCLES

One of 64M US freelancers. Lost a 40-day streak during a client crunch and deleted the app that day (nearly half of streak-holders do unwanted tasks just to keep streaks — until they quit entirely).

Stays because: weekly quotas are workload-shaped, the floor doubles as a week-at-a-glance, and pay-once matches variable income (47% canceled a subscription last year).

Spread-Thin Sam

20–26 · STUDENT · ADHD + SPOONIE OVERLAP

~16% of college students report ADHD; schedule changes every semester, energy changes every day. Habitica's HP loss turned a bad brain day into punishment; Finch's pet became a chore.

Stays because: chips give partial credit ("5 of 8 counts"), blocks need no feeding, forgiveness is structural not purchasable, and $5.99 is student money.

6 · Pricing position

$5.99 one-time (store builds), free installable web app as the funnel. Evidence: >50% of in-app purchases sit between $0.49–$9.99; Streaks' decade at $5.99; 89% of consumers underestimate subscription spend ($273 actual vs $86 perceived/mo) — "pay once, keep forever" is an economic and emotional fit for an audience taxed by forgotten renewals.[6],[16],[19] GAP No survey-grade ADHD-community pricing data exists; run a 100-person community poll before locking store copy.
