E1 · Day 0 — the welcome

Send: immediatelySubject: Your blocks are waitingPreview: One bucket is enough to start.

Hey — Raina here. I built Kachunk because every habit app I tried eventually made me feel bad, and I don't need an app for that.

Here's the whole system: your week is a pile of physical blocks. Each block is one time you said you'd do the thing. Do it, drag it into its bucket — kachunk. That's it. That's the app.

Start with one bucket. Seriously. Move ×3 or Water ×7. You can add more once the first one feels good.

Set up your first bucket

PS — you can throw the blocks around. It's allowed. It's encouraged.

E2 · Day 1 — the no-streaks promise

Send: +1 day, morningSubject: There is no streak to breakPreview: One bad Tuesday can't touch you here.

Quick thing you should know about Kachunk, because it's the opposite of every tracker you've deleted:

Nothing resets. Nothing turns red. There is no streak.

Your goals are weekly, so a chaotic Tuesday just means the blocks wait for Wednesday. At the end of the week, Kachunk asks "did you do it?" — you tap what happened, and the week restacks. You're never behind; you're just mid-week.

See your board

PS — miss a whole week? It goes in Receipts as a quiet little row, not a crime scene.

E3 · Day 3 — chips & the today line

Send: +3 daysSubject: The trick for habits that happen 8 times a dayPreview: Drag it below the line and watch.

Two power moves most people find by accident:

1 · The today line. Drag blocks below the dotted line to plan today. Kachunk budgets your day in slots so you can't overpromise — it'll tell you when today is full.

2 · Chips. Water ×8 isn't one moment, it's eight tiny ones. Turn on chips mode for a bucket, drop its block below the line, and it shatters into little chips. Bank them one at a time; hit your threshold and the whole block counts — confetti included.

Try shattering a block

E4 · Day 6 — your first sweep

Send: +6 days, eveningSubject: Tomorrow, your week asks one questionPreview: "Did you do it?" — that's the whole ceremony.

Your first week sweep is coming up. Here's how it works, so it isn't a surprise:

The board dims, and you tap every block that actually happened — it drops home. Anything you did but forgot to log? Tap it now, it still counts. Then hit Restack and a fresh week of blocks tumbles onto the board.

Whatever the number is, it's a real number about your real week. That's worth more than a perfect streak you had to lie to keep.

Open Kachunk

PS — after the sweep, check Receipts. Watching the weekly rows stack up is the quiet superpower of this whole app.
