App-store metadata plan · v1

The listing, written before the wrapper.

Path to stores: the PWA ships today; PWABuilder → Google Play (TWA) and an iOS shell (Capacitor) follow after name clearance. Both stores get the same story: buckets not calendars, pay once, zero shame. Price: $5.99 one-time. Web app stays free as the funnel.

Pricing — the recommendation

Charge $5.99, once. Not a subscription, not freemium tiers — one price, said loudly. Here's the reasoning:

AnchorWhat it tells us
Streaks (Apple Design Award) — $5.99 one-timeThe proven ceiling for a beloved indie habit tracker with no server costs. Kachunk's economics are identical (local-first, no backend).
Subscription competitors — Tiimo ~$55/yr, Finch ~$40–70/yr"Pay once" is the loudest differentiator in the category. The ADHD community calls subscription pile-up the "ADHD tax" — one-time pricing is positioning, not just revenue.
Below $4.99Reads disposable; impulse-buy price attracts churn reviews. Don't.
Above $7.99Needs sync + widgets + wrapper polish to justify. Revisit after those ship.

The ladder: launch week $3.99 ("founding price", Product Hunt exclusive) → settle at $5.99 → raise to $7.99 only after Google Drive sync + home-screen widget ship and the store rating holds ≥4.5. The free web PWA stays free forever as the funnel — it's the demo, the share link, and the trust builder. Never gate existing features behind a later price.

Description (long)

Calendars lie to you. You block out 7am gym, life happens at 6:45, and now the whole week feels broken. Kachunk doesn't do calendars. You get BLOCKS — chunky, tossable, weirdly satisfying blocks — and BUCKETS, one per commitment. Gym 3× a week? Three blocks. Do a gym thing (any day! that's the point!), drag a block in. KACHUNK. That sound is the whole app. WHY IT WORKS WHEN PLANNERS DIDN'T • A week can absorb a bad Tuesday. A timeline can't. • No streaks — nothing to break, nothing to mourn at 11:59pm. • Chips give partial credit: 5 of 8 glasses still counts. Good enough is recorded as good enough. • The Roundup: ONE notification a day, at a time you pick. When the week ends, Kachunk asks what actually happened — it never resets you into the void. • Receipts: your proof, in dots. In-progress counts as done on the overview, because it is. BUILT CALM Designed with ADHD brains in mind: a quiet canvas, six soft colors, big type, one action per screen, and physics you can fidget with. Toss the blocks. Stack them. It's allowed. FOUR VOICES Plain (just the facts) · Zesty (a little cheek — default) · Extra Spicy (roast me) · Sugar Rush (maximum pompoms). YOUR DATA IS YOURS Everything lives on your device. Coming soon: optional sync to your own Google Drive — your account, your file, no server of ours anywhere. $5.99. Once. That's it. No subscription, no "premium" blocks, no guilt.

Ratings, reviews & featuring

Review prompt timing: after the user's first completed Restack — their success moment — never on first launch. Apple featuring pitch: inclusivity/ND-first angle via the App Store promote form; windows: ADHD Awareness Month (October), GAAD (May). Tiimo's Design Award nomination proves the lane exists. Claim discipline: "designed with ADHD brains in mind" everywhere; no treatment claims anywhere in store copy.

Wrapper checklist (post-clearance)
